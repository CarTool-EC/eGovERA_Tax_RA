OV-Information Base ()
NARRATIVE: The OV-Information Base view extends Organisational view and provides a comprehensive framework for leveraging business objects and grouping ABBs to support the delivery of digital public services. The view consists of two main elements: 'Information' and 'Business Agnostic Business Information Base'. 'Information' is a Business Object ABB that describes business facts, assets, or opinions that are exchanged in the context of a digital public service to support its delivery. This information is enacted via interpretation rules (expressed in an ontology) of data and considering knowledge of a given context. Examples include an invoice, a medical prescription, a driving license. The 'Business Agnostic Business Information Base' is a Grouping ABB that serves as a catalogue for various Business Object ABBs. These include 'Payment', 'Transaction', 'Identification', 'Qualification', 'Audit trail', 'Access rights', 'Profile', 'Message', 'Availability', 'Digital Signature', 'Document', 'Security Measure', 'Location', 'Invoice', 'Contracting authority', 'Economic operator', 'Digital Asset', 'Credential', 'Data Quality', and 'Distribution list'. Each of these represents a specific type of business information that is essential for the functioning of the digital public service. The view also includes a 'Customs Information Base', which is another Grouping ABB. This base contains specific Business Object ABBs related to customs operations, such as 'Economic Operators Registration and Identification number (EORI)', 'Free circulation requirement information', 'Laboratories information', 'Threads and fraudulent actions Information', 'Duties calculation Information', 'Imports information', 'Combined nomenclature', 'Workforce information', 'Classification of goods Information', 'Temporary storage Information', 'Exports information', 'Special Procedures Information', 'Binding Tariffs Information', 'Guarantee Information', 'Economic Operators Information', 'Customs partners Information', 'Economic value Information', 'Internal logistic Information', and 'Origin of goods Information'. The relations between these elements are not explicitly defined in the JSON, but it can be inferred that the 'Business Agnostic Business Information Base' and 'Customs Information Base' are subsets of the 'Information' element, as they provide specific types of information needed for the digital public service. The motivation behind this structure is to provide a comprehensive and organized view of all the information elements involved in the service, facilitating their management and utilization.
