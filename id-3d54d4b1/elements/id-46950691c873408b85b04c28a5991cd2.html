Tangible assets registry management for mobile assets ()
Definition: Tangible Assets (Mobile Assets) Registry Management is an IT concept that involves the use of software or systems to track, manage, and maintain a record of an organization's tangible or mobile assets. These assets could range from computers, vehicles, equipment, to any other physical assets that can be moved around. Source: Investopedia Source reference: https://www.investopedia.com/terms/t/tangibleasset.asp Additional information: The management of tangible assets is crucial for any organization as it helps in tracking the location, status, and maintenance history of the assets, thereby aiding in decision-making processes related to asset utilization, maintenance, and procurement. This IT concept is particularly useful in large organizations with numerous assets spread across multiple locations. The software or systems used for this purpose provide features like asset tagging, barcode or QR code scanning, real-time tracking, reporting, and analytics. They also help in compliance with various regulatory requirements related to asset management. Example: Examples of application include a logistics company using a mobile asset management system to track the location and status of its fleet of trucks. A manufacturing company might use it to keep track of its machinery and equipment, their maintenance schedules, and their performance. A large corporation could use it to manage its IT assets like computers, servers, and other hardware.
