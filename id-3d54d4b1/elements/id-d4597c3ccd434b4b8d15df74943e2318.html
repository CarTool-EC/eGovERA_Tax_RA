Business Agnostic Digital Workplace Enablers ()
Definition: Bussiness Agnostic Digital Workplace Enablers ABB is a Grouping that covers a range of software application services designed to enhance productivity, collaboration, and learning in a digital workplace. These services are business-agnostic, meaning they can be applied across various industries and business types. They include Training and Learning Management, Learning Platform System, Remote Collaboration and Communication, and Digital Collaboration. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Bussiness Agnostic Digital Workplace Enablers ABB includes four key elements. The 'Training and Learning Management' element is an application service that helps organizations efficiently plan, administer, deliver, and track training and learning activities. The 'Learning Platform System' is a software application service that provides a comprehensive platform for managing and delivering learning content and experiences. The 'Remote Collaboration and Communication' is a software application service that enables individuals or teams to collaborate and communicate effectively, regardless of their physical locations. Lastly, the 'Digital Collaboration' is a broad term that encompasses various tools and strategies used to facilitate collaboration in a digital environment. Example: Examples of application of these elements could include a multinational corporation using a Learning Platform System to deliver standardized training to employees around the world, a remote team using Remote Collaboration and Communication tools to work together on a project, or an organization using a Training and Learning Management system to track employee progress and certifications.
