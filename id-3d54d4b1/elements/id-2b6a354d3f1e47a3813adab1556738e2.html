Decision 93/329/EEC ()
DESCRIPTION: "Euopean Legal Act approved on behalf of the European Economic Community (now the EU) the Convention on Temporary Admission* agreed at Istanbul on 26 June 1990 and its annexes, subject to certain conditions. The aim of the convention is to facilitate temporary admission by simplifying and harmonising procedures through the adoption of standardised model papers such as international customs documents with international security, thereby contributing to the development of international trade."

dct:descriptionThe Decision 93/329/EEC refers to the conclusion of the Istanbul Convention on Temporary Admission and the acceptance of its annexes. The Istanbul Convention, also known as the Convention relating to temporary admission, is an international agreement aimed at facilitating the temporary admission of goods into a customs territory without the payment of import duties or taxes. It provides a framework for the temporary admission of goods for specific purposes such as exhibitions, fairs, and other similar events. The convention sets out the conditions, procedures, and requirements for temporary admission, ensuring the proper control and management of goods during their temporary stay in a customs territory.

skos:exampleAn example of applying the Istanbul Convention is when a company based in the European Union wants to showcase their products at an international trade fair in a non-EU country. By utilizing the provisions of the convention, the company can temporarily export their goods to the trade fair without paying import duties or taxes. This allows them to promote their products, explore new markets, and potentially secure business opportunities without incurring additional costs or administrative burdens.

skos:noteThe Decision 93/329/EEC signifies the European Union's commitment to the Istanbul Convention on Temporary Admission and its annexes. It establishes the legal basis for the EU's participation in the convention and ensures the harmonization of temporary admission procedures within the EU member states. The decision aims to simplify and streamline the temporary admission process, promoting international trade and facilitating the movement of goods for specific purposes.
