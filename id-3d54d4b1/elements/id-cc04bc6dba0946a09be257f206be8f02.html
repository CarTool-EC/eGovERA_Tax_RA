Regulation (EEC) No 2658/87 ()
DESCRIPTION: European Legal Act introducing goods nomenclature, hereinafter called the ‘combined nomenclature’, or in abbreviated form ‘CN’, is hereby established to meet, at one and the same time, the requirements both of the Common Customs Tariff and of the external trade statistics of the Community.

dct:descriptionRegulation (EEC) No 2658/87 is a legal act of the European Economic Community (EEC) that establishes the tariff and statistical nomenclature for goods imported or exported within the European Union (EU). It provides a standardized classification system for goods based on the Harmonized System (HS) codes, facilitating trade and ensuring consistency in customs procedures across EU member states.

skos:exampleFor example, if a company wants to export a shipment of mobile phones from Germany to France, they need to consult Regulation (EEC) No 2658/87 to determine the appropriate HS code for mobile phones. This code will be used by customs authorities to assess the applicable customs duties and to gather accurate trade statistics. Compliance with this regulation ensures smooth customs clearance and avoids potential disputes or delays in international trade.

skos:noteThis regulation serves as the basis for the classification of goods in the EU customs territory. It defines the rules for determining the correct HS code for each product, which is essential for customs duties, trade statistics, and other regulatory purposes. The regulation is regularly updated to reflect changes in international trade and to ensure harmonization with global standards.
