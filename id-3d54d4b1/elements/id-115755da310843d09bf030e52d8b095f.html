Economic activities catalogue ()
Definition: The Economic Activities Catalogue is a comprehensive list that classifies and describes all the possible economic activities that can be performed by businesses and individuals. It serves as a reference tool for economic analysis, policy-making, and business decision-making. Source: International Standard Industrial Classification of All Economic Activities (ISIC) Source reference: https://unstats.un.org/unsd/publication/seriesm/seriesm_4rev4e.pdf Additional information: The Economic Activities Catalogue is a crucial tool for understanding and analyzing the economic landscape. It provides a systematic classification of all economic activities, allowing for easy comparison and analysis. The catalogue is typically structured according to sectors, such as agriculture, manufacturing, services, etc., and further divided into sub-sectors. It is used by economists, policy-makers, business analysts, and others to understand economic trends, make policy decisions, and guide business strategy. The catalogue is typically updated periodically to reflect changes in the economy and emerging industries.
