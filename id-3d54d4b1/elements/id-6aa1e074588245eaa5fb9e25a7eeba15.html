Tax Legislation on Behavioural Interoperability (ENBI) ()
Definition: Tax Legislation on Behavioural Interoperability (ENBI) ABB is a grouping that covers the legal and regulatory aspects of tax laws and their impact on the interoperability of different systems and behaviours. It includes the study of how tax laws influence the interaction between different systems, entities or processes and how they can be designed or modified to promote better interoperability. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The ENBI ABB is an essential tool for policymakers, tax professionals, and IT specialists. It helps in understanding the complex interplay between tax legislation and behavioural interoperability. This includes how tax laws can affect the way different systems interact, how data is shared and processed, and how different entities cooperate to achieve common goals. The ENBI ABB also explores how tax laws can be designed or modified to promote better interoperability, efficiency, and compliance. This can involve changes to the tax code, the introduction of new technologies, or the development of new procedures and protocols. Example: For instance, a country might modify its tax laws to encourage the use of a particular type of software or technology. This could make it easier for different systems to interact and share data, leading to greater efficiency and compliance. Alternatively, a country might introduce new tax laws that require certain types of data to be shared or processed in a particular way. This could promote interoperability by ensuring that all systems are using the same standards and protocols.
