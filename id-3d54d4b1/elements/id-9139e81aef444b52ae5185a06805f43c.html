Taxpayer registry management for public administrations ()
Definition: Taxpayer Registry Management is an IT concept that involves the use of digital systems to manage, organize, and maintain the records of taxpayers in public administrations. It is a critical component in the efficient collection of taxes and the provision of public services. Source: Investopedia Source reference: https://www.investopedia.com/terms/t/taxpayer.asp Additional information: Taxpayer Registry Management systems are designed to streamline the process of tax collection by maintaining a comprehensive and up-to-date record of all taxpayers. These systems typically include features for recording taxpayer identification numbers, tracking tax payments and liabilities, and managing taxpayer accounts. They may also include tools for auditing and compliance checks, as well as interfaces for taxpayers to manage their own accounts. The use of such systems can greatly enhance the efficiency and effectiveness of public administrations in managing their tax base. Example: Examples of application include the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) in the United States and Her Majesty's Revenue and Customs (HMRC) in the United Kingdom. Both agencies use sophisticated IT systems to manage their taxpayer registries, enabling them to track tax payments, identify non-compliance, and provide online services to taxpayers.
