Taxpayer registry management for individuals ()
Definition: Taxpayer (Individuals) Registry Management is an IT concept that involves the use of technology to manage, organize, and maintain a database of individual taxpayers. It includes the collection, storage, and processing of taxpayer data such as personal information, income details, tax liabilities, and payment history. Source: Investopedia Source reference: https://www.investopedia.com/terms/t/taxpayer.asp Additional information: Taxpayer (Individuals) Registry Management is a crucial aspect of tax administration. It involves the use of IT systems to manage a comprehensive database of individual taxpayers. The system collects, stores, and processes a wide range of taxpayer data, including personal details (like name, address, and social security number), income details, tax liabilities, and payment history. This data is used for various purposes, such as calculating tax liabilities, tracking tax payments, enforcing tax compliance, and providing taxpayer services. The system also facilitates the communication between tax authorities and taxpayers, making tax administration more efficient and effective. Example: Examples of application include the IRS's Individual Master File (IMF) system in the United States, which maintains a record of all individual taxpayers in the country. Another example is the Personal Tax Account system in the United Kingdom, which allows taxpayers to manage their tax affairs online. These systems enable tax authorities to efficiently manage taxpayer data, calculate tax liabilities, enforce tax compliance, and provide taxpayer services.
