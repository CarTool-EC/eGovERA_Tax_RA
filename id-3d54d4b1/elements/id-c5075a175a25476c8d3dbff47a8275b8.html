Tax Ontologies Catalogue Catalogue ()
Definition: Tax Ontologies Catalogue Catalogue ABB is a grouping that covers the comprehensive collection of harmonised tax data ontologies. It is a structured representation of tax-related concepts and their interrelationships, designed to facilitate the exchange of tax data across different systems and platforms. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Tax Ontologies Catalogue Catalogue ABB is an essential tool for tax professionals, data scientists, and IT specialists working in the field of taxation. It provides a standardised framework for the representation of tax data, enabling efficient data exchange and interoperability between different tax systems. The catalogue includes a wide range of tax ontologies, each of which represents a specific aspect of tax data. These ontologies are harmonised, meaning that they are designed to be consistent with each other and with international tax standards. This harmonisation facilitates the integration of tax data from different sources and the development of cross-platform tax applications. Example: For instance, one of the ontologies in the Tax Ontologies Catalogue Catalogue ABB might represent the concept of 'taxable income'. This ontology would define what constitutes taxable income, how it is calculated, and how it relates to other tax concepts. By using this ontology, a tax software application could accurately calculate a user's taxable income, regardless of the specific tax system or platform the application is designed for.
