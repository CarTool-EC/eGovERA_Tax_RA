Tax Semantic Agreements Solutions ()
Definition: Tax Semantic Agreements Solutions (TSAS) is a grouping that covers a range of elements related to tax and tariff nomenclature, World Trade Organization (WTO) nomenclature agreements, and tax harmonisation agreements. These elements are primarily contracts that define the terms and conditions of these tax-related agreements. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Tax Semantic Agreements Solutions (TSAS) is a comprehensive catalogue of elements that are crucial in the field of taxation and trade. It includes 'Tax and Tariff Combined Nomenclature', which is a contract that outlines the combined nomenclature of taxes and tariffs. Another element is the 'WTO Nomenclature Agreements', which are contracts that detail the nomenclature agreements of the World Trade Organization. Lastly, the 'Tax Harmonisation Agreements' are contracts that define the agreements for harmonising tax policies and regulations. These elements are essential for understanding and implementing tax-related agreements in a global context. Example: For instance, a multinational corporation may refer to the 'Tax and Tariff Combined Nomenclature' to understand the tax and tariff classifications in different countries. Similarly, a country may refer to the 'WTO Nomenclature Agreements' to align its trade policies with WTO standards. Moreover, countries aiming to harmonise their tax policies may refer to the 'Tax Harmonisation Agreements'.
