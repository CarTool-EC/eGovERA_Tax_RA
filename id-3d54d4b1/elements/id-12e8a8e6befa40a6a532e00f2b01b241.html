WTO trade and goods exchange metadata ()
Definition: The WTO Trade and Goods Exchange Metadata is a comprehensive dataset that provides detailed information about the exchange of goods and services between WTO member countries. It includes data on import and export volumes, trade balances, tariffs, and other relevant trade indicators. Source: World Trade Organization Source reference: https://www.wto.org/ Additional information: The WTO Trade and Goods Exchange Metadata is a crucial resource for economists, policymakers, and researchers studying international trade. It provides a detailed picture of the flow of goods and services between countries, allowing for in-depth analysis of trade patterns, trends, and impacts. The data is collected from official sources in each WTO member country and is standardized to ensure comparability. It covers a wide range of goods and services, from agricultural products to high-tech goods and services. The metadata includes information on the quantity and value of goods and services traded, the countries involved in the trade, the tariffs applied, and other relevant indicators. It is updated regularly to reflect the most recent trade data. Example: Examples of application of the WTO Trade and Goods Exchange Metadata include: 1) Analysis of global trade patterns and trends, 2) Assessment of the impact of trade policies and tariffs on the flow of goods and services, 3) Evaluation of a country's trade performance and competitiveness, 4) Research on the relationship between trade and economic growth, 5) Forecasting future trade flows and trends.
