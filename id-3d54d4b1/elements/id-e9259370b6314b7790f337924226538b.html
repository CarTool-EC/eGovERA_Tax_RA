Tax Digital Public Services Catalogue ()
Definition: Tax Digital Public Services Catalogue is a grouping that covers a wide range of digital public services related to taxation. These services include tax calculation and payment, tax returns control, dispute management, registry management for various tax-related entities and assets, anti-fraud management, internal auditing, and more. The catalogue is designed to provide a comprehensive overview of digital services available for managing different aspects of taxation. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Tax Digital Public Services Catalogue is a comprehensive list of digital public services related to taxation. It includes services like 'MyTaxes', 'Tax calendar', 'Tax returns control', 'Tax Pre-Audit and Due-Diligence management', 'Tax professionals and agents registry management', 'Dispute registry management', and many more. Each service in the catalogue has a specific role in the management of taxes, ranging from the calculation and payment of different types of taxes, to the management of registries for taxpayers, tax professionals, tangible and intangible assets, and more. The catalogue also includes services for managing disputes, conducting internal audits, and managing anti-fraud activities. Example: For instance, the 'MyTaxes' service in the catalogue might be a digital platform where taxpayers can view and manage their tax information, make payments, and submit tax returns. The 'Tax calendar' service could be a digital calendar that provides important tax dates and deadlines to help taxpayers plan ahead. The 'Tax returns control' service might be a system for monitoring and managing the submission of tax returns, while the 'Tax Pre-Audit and Due-Diligence management' service could be a tool for conducting pre-audit checks and managing due diligence processes in tax matters.
