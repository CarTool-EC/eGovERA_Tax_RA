Tax Datasets Catalogue ()
Definition: Tax Datasets Catalogue ABB is a grouping that covers a variety of metadata and catalogues related to taxation. It includes tax statistical metadata, public tax metadata, taxpayer behavior history metadata, business sector catalogue, and WTO trade and goods exchange metadata. These elements provide comprehensive information on different aspects of taxation, from public tax data to specific details about taxpayer behavior and international trade. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Tax Datasets Catalogue ABB is a comprehensive collection of metadata and catalogues that provide detailed information on various aspects of taxation. The 'Tax statistical metadata' provides statistical data related to taxation. The 'Public tax metadata' provides information on public tax data. The 'Taxpayer behavior history metadata' provides information on the behavior of taxpayers over time. The 'Business Sector catalogue' provides a catalogue of different business sectors for reference. The 'WTO trade and goods exchange metadata' provides information on trade and goods exchange as per the World Trade Organization. Each of these elements plays a crucial role in understanding and analyzing taxation data. Example: For instance, the 'Tax statistical metadata' can be used by researchers to analyze tax trends over time. The 'Public tax metadata' can be used by the public to understand the tax data released by the government. The 'Taxpayer behavior history metadata' can be used by tax authorities to understand taxpayer behavior and compliance. The 'Business Sector catalogue' can be used by businesses to identify their sector for tax purposes. The 'WTO trade and goods exchange metadata' can be used by international trade analysts to understand the impact of taxes on trade and goods exchange.
